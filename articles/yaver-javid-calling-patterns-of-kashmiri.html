Called Patterns Of Kashmiri
Author : Yaver Javid

Calling or directly addressing a person in Kashmiri is indicated through the addition of a suffix to the end of the name or addressed word. If the word ends in a vowel, the vowel is usually dropped before the suffix is attached. For masculine addressees, the suffix -o is used. For feminine addressees, the suffixes -ï and -ä are used. Preference between the two is often speaker-dependent, though many words permit only one of the two forms. For example:

- mummy → mummä, not *mummï
- zeba → zëbï, not *zëbä

Southern Kashmiri speakers frequently generalize the masculine suffix -o to both masculine and feminine addressees, though they still understand and distinguish the feminine forms. In general, stems ending in j, m, or y tend to tolerate both -ï and -ä. In most other phonological environments, only -ï is accepted. However, lexical exceptions exist at the word level.

These suffixes also attach themselves to some verbs and some particles as well where they indicate that the speaker wants a response. Similar to how calling somebody asks for a response.

Cxelziha means "You should have ran away."

One observation I had after realising it, was about the words "tä", "teo" & "tï". These are used when the recipient doesn't answer for some reason. By uttering one of them one demands a response.
